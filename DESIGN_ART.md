# Elemental Crystal TCG — Art Direction Guide

## Core Vision

Elemental Crystal is a collectible card game set in a magical crystal-powered world populated entirely by animals, magical beasts, dragons, and anthropomorphic creatures.

Humans do not exist.

The world is built around living crystal energy that shapes ecosystems, creatures, environments, and magic.

Everything ultimately traces back to crystal power.

The game should feel magical, adventurous, collectible, optimistic, and full of discovery.

Players should feel like they are exploring a vibrant world of creatures rather than participating in a medieval war.

---

# World Theme

Ancient crystal formations grow naturally throughout the world.

Examples:

* Crystal forests
* Crystal caverns
* Floating crystal islands
* Crystal mountains
* Crystal rivers
* Crystal ruins
* Crystal storms

Crystal energy behaves almost like a living force of nature.

Creatures adapt to crystal environments and often incorporate crystal traits into their appearance.

---

# Visual Pillars

Every piece of artwork should emphasize:

* Glowing crystal energy
* Faceted gemstone structures
* Strong focal readability
* Vibrant magical lighting
* Clean silhouettes
* High contrast focal points
* Painterly fantasy rendering
* Premium collectible card quality

Crystal energy should feel alive.

Magic should appear to originate from crystal formations rather than abstract elemental forces.

---

# Creature Philosophy

Creatures are the heart of the game.

Players should become attached to creatures the same way players become attached to Pokémon, Digimon, or monster companions.

Every creature should feel:

* Distinct
* Memorable
* Cute, cool, or majestic
* Collectible
* Recognizable from silhouette

---

# Character Restrictions

## Forbidden

No:

* Humans
* Human knights
* Human mages
* Human soldiers
* Human kings
* Human villagers
* Elves
* Dwarves
* Orcs
* Goblins
* Halflings

The world belongs entirely to creatures.

---

# Allowed Creature Types

## Pure Animals

Examples:

* Wolf
* Fox
* Bear
* Owl
* Deer
* Falcon
* Tiger
* Rabbit
* Turtle

These may possess magical crystal traits.

---

## Animal Heroes

Anthropomorphic animals.

Examples:

* Fox ranger
* Owl scholar
* Bear guardian
* Rabbit explorer
* Falcon knight
* Turtle defender

Animal identity must always dominate.

The design should feel like an animal that gained intelligence, not a human wearing an animal costume.

### Preferred Ratios

* 70-90% animal
* 10-30% humanoid influence

Good examples:

* Zootopia
* Robin Hood (Disney)
* Redwall
* Beastars silhouette language

Avoid:

* Human body with animal head
* Human anatomy with animal skin
* Human facial proportions

---

## Dragons

Dragons are a major creature family.

Examples:

* Crystal Wyrms
* Ruby Dragons
* Sapphire Dragons
* Emerald Drakes
* Quartz Hatchlings

Dragons should be expressive, collectible, and memorable.

---

## Crystal Beasts

Creatures naturally infused with crystal energy.

Examples:

* Crystal Wolves
* Crystal Stags
* Crystal Lions
* Crystal Serpents
* Crystal Tortoises

Crystals should appear as a natural biological feature.

Avoid attaching random crystal spikes without integrating them into the creature design.

---

# Creature Appeal Target

Creatures should feel:

* Charming
* Expressive
* Confident
* Adventurous
* Heroic
* Collectible

Not:

* Grotesque
* Horror-themed
* Hyper-realistic
* Excessively violent
* Grimdark

The player should want to collect them.

---

# Eyes and Expressions

Eyes are extremely important.

Preferred traits:

* Large readable eyes
* Strong expression
* Clear personality
* Memorable facial features

Faces should be immediately recognizable at card size.

---

# Creature Card Artwork

## Purpose

Creature cards showcase a character, beast, dragon, spirit, construct, or crystal creature.

The creature is always the hero of the artwork.

Players should remember the creature itself.

---

## Composition

### Top-Weighted Layout

Creature artwork must assume that the lower portion of the illustration will be partially covered by card UI.

The primary focal point should occupy the upper half of the image.

Important visual information must remain visible after text overlays are added.

---

### Face Priority

The face should usually occupy the upper-middle region.

The creature's face is the primary recognition anchor.

Players should be able to identify a card from its face alone.

---

### Preferred Framing

Best:

* Hero shot
* Bust shot
* Three-quarter shot

Acceptable:

* Full body if the face remains high in frame

Avoid:

* Tiny subjects
* Distant camera angles
* Face near bottom of frame

---

## Environment Usage

Environment exists to support the creature.

Examples:

* Crystal forests
* Crystal caves
* Floating crystal islands
* Crystal ruins
* Crystal canyons
* Crystal mountains

The creature should remain the dominant visual element.

---

# Skill Card Artwork

## Philosophy

Skill cards depict magical events.

The magical phenomenon is the hero.

Not the caster.

Players should remember the effect.

---

## Composition

### Center-Weighted Layout

The magical event occupies the center of the artwork.

The effect should dominate the image.

Examples:

* Ruby Crystal Burst
* Emerald Dash
* Sapphire Renewal
* Quartz Prism Collapse

---

## Character Usage

Characters are optional.

If present, they should only provide scale.

The skill itself remains the focal point.

---

## Visual Scale

The effect should occupy approximately 60-80% of the artwork.

The magical event should remain readable even at thumbnail size.

---

## Motion

Skills should feel energetic.

Use:

* Crystal shards
* Energy trails
* Shockwaves
* Swirling particles
* Crystal fractures
* Explosive illumination

Avoid static scenes.

---

# Artifact Card Artwork

## Philosophy

Artifacts showcase magical objects.

The object is the hero.

Examples:

* Crystal Sword
* Crystal Crown
* Crystal Orb
* Crystal Compass
* Crystal Relic

---

## Composition

### Center-Weighted Layout

The artifact should dominate the image.

The environment should support the object.

Think of magical product photography.

---

# Crystal Language Rules

Every artwork should contain evidence of the crystal-powered world.

Use:

* Crystal structures
* Crystal shards
* Faceted surfaces
* Crystal veins
* Crystal cores
* Crystal formations
* Prism lighting

Even non-crystal creatures should feel like they evolved within a crystal ecosystem.

# Lighting Direction

Magic emits light.

Preferred hierarchy:

1. Near-white crystal core
2. Affinity color glow
3. Saturated crystal body
4. Darker supporting environment

The focal point should often be the brightest area of the image.

Avoid flat lighting.

Avoid dull ambient illumination.

---

# Rendering Style

Painterly fantasy illustration.

Characteristics:

* Premium TCG quality
* Rich detail
* Strong focal readability
* Soft painterly rendering
* Vibrant magical lighting
* Clear silhouettes
* Family-friendly appeal

Not:

* Photorealistic
* Anime
* Comic book
* Cel-shaded
* Grimdark
* Horror

---

# Emotional Direction

Every card should communicate one or more of the following:

* Wonder
* Adventure
* Discovery
* Courage
* Friendship
* Growth
* Magic

The world should feel like a place players want to explore and collect rather than conquer.

---

# Brand Consistency

The crystal resource icons establish the game's visual identity.

All supporting assets should follow the same language:

* Crystal-based effects
* Glowing internal illumination
* Faceted gemstone forms
* Bright focal highlights
* Clean readable silhouettes

UI, card art, VFX, status effects, abilities, and card illustrations should feel like they belong to the same crystal-powered universe.
