# DESIGN_SWIFT_REWORK_V5

## Goal

Replace COMBO as SWIFT's primary identity.

New SWIFT fantasy:
- Tempo
- Trickery
- Stealth
- Theft
- Bounce
- Temporary Energy manipulation

The archetype should feel closer to Hearthstone Rogue than a combo-spell deck.

---

## Core Identity

### MIGHTY
Win through raw power.

### VITAL
Win through sustain and value.

### SWIFT
Win through tempo advantage.

SWIFT bends the rules of Energy and positioning to gain short-term advantages.

---

## New SWIFT Keyword: Ambush

Ambush: gains a bonus the first time it attacks.

Example:

Shadowblade Stalker
Cost 2
2/2
Ambush: Gain +2 Attack when attacking for the first time.

---

## New SWIFT Keyword: Preparation

Preparation cards reduce the cost of future Actions.

Example:

Preparation
Cost 1
Your next Action costs 2 less this turn.

---

## New SWIFT Keyword: Opportunist

Rewards playing efficiently without requiring Combo.

Example:

Opportunist Cutpurse
2/3
Whenever you play your second card each turn, gain 1 temporary Energy.

---

## Signature Mechanics

### Bounce
Return minions to hand.

### Stealth
Protect fragile threats.

### Theft
Copy or steal resources.

### Temporary Energy
Gain Energy this turn only.

SWIFT should be the only archetype allowed to regularly generate temporary Energy.

---

## Proposed Invoker Progression

### Tier 3
First Action played each turn costs 1 less.

### Tier 6
The first time each turn you play two cards, gain 1 temporary Energy.

### Tier 9
The first Action each turn costs 2 less.

---

## Expected Matchup Shape

SWIFT vs MIGHTY: Slightly favored for SWIFT.

SWIFT disrupts expensive threats through bounce and tempo.

Estimated: 52% SWIFT / 48% MIGHTY.

SWIFT vs VITAL: Nearly even.

Stealth pressure prevents VITAL from comfortably stabilizing.

Estimated: 48% SWIFT / 52% VITAL.

SWIFT vs SWIFT: Skill and tempo driven mirror.

Estimated first-player advantage reduced compared to current design.

---

## Design Rule

Avoid combining card draw and Energy cheating on the same card.

Historically this creates runaway combo decks and undermines the tempo identity.
