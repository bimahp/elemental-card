# DESIGN_CORE — Elemental TCG: Core Design

## High Concept

A digital Trading Card Game for Roblox combining:

- Long-term monster progression and mastery
- Deckbuilding and strategic resource management
- Fast, readable board states
- Social TCG store atmosphere

Players collect monsters, level them up through gameplay, unlock additional skills and build options, and construct decks around elemental resource engines.

Deeper than most Roblox card games. Simpler than Yu-Gi-Oh.

---

## Design Philosophy

The game is built around:

```
Board Presence
+
Resource Engines
+
Monster Progression
```

NOT:

```
Huge Mana Curves
Type Advantages
Stat Inflation
```

Players win by building a better board, managing resources efficiently, and mastering their monsters — not by drawing one overpowered boss card.

---

## Core Game Loop

1. Build a 24-card deck around one element
2. Summon monsters to generate elemental resources
3. Spend resources on skills, supports, and stronger monsters
4. Reduce the opponent's Core HP to 0

---

## Resource System

### Energy

Represents player tempo.

- Gain +1 Energy at the start of every turn
- Energy persists between turns (unspent Energy is saved)
- Energy is consumed when used

Energy pays for:
- Summoning Monsters
- Playing Skills
- Playing Supports
- Equipping Equipment

Example:
```
Turn 1: Gain 1 Energy → Total: 1
Turn 2: Gain 1 Energy → Total: 2
Turn 3: Spend 1 to summon → Total: 2
```

---

### Element Resources

Generated by monsters on the board each turn.

Current elements:
- Fire
- Nature
- Lightning
- Water

Rules:
- All bench and active monsters generate elements each turn
- Element resources persist between turns
- Consumed when used
- Each element has its own separate pool

Element resources pay for:
- Summoning stronger monsters (Level 2+)
- Casting skill cards
- Activating equipment effects
- Playing supports

---

## Element Identities

Elements do NOT deal bonus damage to each other. No weakness or resistance multipliers exist. Advantages emerge from strategy, not a type chart.

### Fire
- **Theme:** Aggression, Burst, Resource Conversion
- **Playstyle:** Generate Fire, convert it into damage, end games quickly
- **Key cards:** Fireball, Dragon Breath, Inferno

### Nature
- **Theme:** Growth, Healing, Durability
- **Playstyle:** Maintain board presence, heal allies, outlast opponents
- **Key cards:** Regrowth, Thorn Armor, Elder Treant

### Lightning
- **Theme:** Tempo, Speed, Pressure
- **Playstyle:** Cheap efficient effects, multiple actions, early aggression
- **Key cards:** Zap, Chain Bolt, Thunder Hawk

### Water
- **Theme:** Control, Disruption, Patience
- **Playstyle:** Interrupt enemy plans, remove resources, reset board progress
- **Key cards:** Tidal Drain, Undertow, Frost Turtle

## Card Types

### Monster
Placed onto the board. Contains:
- HP
- ATK
- Element generation (per turn)
- Passive effects
- Built-in active skill (usable while on board, costs Element)

### Skill
One-time effect played from hand. Costs Energy + Element. Examples: damage, healing, buffs, control.

### Support
Non-monster utility cards. Subtypes:
- **General Support** — immediate effect (draw, resource gain)
- **Field Support** — persistent effect that stays on board
- **Equipment** — attached to a monster, provides ongoing benefit

---

## Monster Level System

| Level | Energy Cost | Element Cost | Role              |
|-------|-------------|--------------|-------------------|
| 1     | 1           | 0            | Generators, starters |
| 2     | 1           | 1            | Midgame, stats + utility |
| 3     | 1           | 2            | Payoff, strongest tier |

No monsters above Level 3 are planned. The game intentionally avoids large mana curves and boss-level power spikes.

---

## Social Experience

The game takes place inside a 3D TCG store. Players can:
- Open card packs
- Trade cards with other players
- Challenge NPCs
- Duel other players
- Participate in tournaments

Duels take place at tables. Spectators can watch naturally from outside the table area.

---

## Victory Condition

Each player starts with **Core HP: 20**.

- Active Monster must be attacked first
- If Active Monster is defeated, a Bench Monster becomes the new Active
- If a player has no monsters available, attacks target the Core directly
- Reduce opponent Core HP to 0 to win

See [DESIGN_BATTLE.md](DESIGN_BATTLE.md) for full combat rules.
