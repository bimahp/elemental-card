# DESIGN_CORE — Elemental TCG: Core Design

> **⚠️ SUPERSEDED BY v0.3 — 2026-06-07.** Following playtesting, the typed-energy
> system, 5-slot board, and 4-type structure described in this document are
> being replaced by a complete mechanical redesign. See **`DESIGN_CORE_V3.md`**
> for the new specification and `DEV_LOG.md` → Session 9 for the full reasoning.
> This document is kept for historical reference during the rewrite — **do not
> build new work against it.**

## High Concept

A digital card battle game for Roblox combining:

- Monster collection and long-term skill mastery
- Board-based energy engine strategy
- Fast, readable 5v5 board states
- Social TCG store atmosphere

Players collect monsters, master them through play to unlock new skills, configure decks in the collection screen, and battle using typed energy generated by their own board.

Deeper than most Roblox card games. Simpler than traditional TCGs.

---

## Design Philosophy

The game is built around:

```
Board Presence
+
Energy Engine
+
Monster Mastery
```

NOT:

```
Summon Gates / Tribute Costs
Type Advantage Charts
Stat Inflation Through Tiers
```

Players win by building a better board, managing their energy engine, and making smarter skill decisions — not by drawing one unanswerable card.

---

## Battle Types

There are four battle types. Types define what energy a monster generates and what energy its skills cost. Types do **not** have advantages over each other — no type beats another type. Strategic differences emerge from each type's mechanics, not a chart.

| Type   | Color  | Identity                          |
|--------|--------|-----------------------------------|
| MIGHTY | Red    | Power, burst damage, aggression   |
| SWIFT  | Yellow | Speed, card draw, tempo           |
| TOUGH  | Green  | Defense, healing, sustain         |
| VITAL  | Blue   | Control, disruption, energy drain |

---

## Core Game Loop

1. Build a 20-card deck around one battle type
2. Summon monsters (free, one per turn) to generate typed energy
3. Spend energy on monster skills to deal damage and control the board
4. When a monster is killed, its owner's Core HP takes damage equal to that monster's max HP
5. Reduce the opponent's Core HP to 0 to win

---

## Resource System: Typed Energy

There is **one resource type**: Typed Energy.

Each battle type has its own energy pool:
- MIGHTY energy (generated by MIGHTY monsters)
- SWIFT energy (generated by SWIFT monsters)
- TOUGH energy (generated by TOUGH monsters)
- VITAL energy (generated by VITAL monsters)

Typed energy is:
- Generated by monsters on your board at the start of each turn
- Pooled per type (each type has its own running total)
- Persistent between turns — unspent energy carries over
- Consumed when spent on monster skills

### Energy Generation

At the start of each turn (Generate Phase):
- All monsters currently on your board add their listed energy to your pool
- A monster summoned last turn generates this turn
- A monster summoned this turn generates starting next turn

Example:
```
Board: Battle Imp (+1 MIGHTY), Drake Whelp (+2 MIGHTY), Charged Hound (+1 MIGHTY)
Pool before: 1 MIGHTY
Generation: +4 MIGHTY
Pool after: 5 MIGHTY
```

---

## Board

Each player controls **5 open monster slots**.

```
[Slot 1] [Slot 2] [Slot 3] [Slot 4] [Slot 5]
```

- All 5 slots are equivalent — no Active, no Bench
- All monsters on board generate energy each turn
- All monsters can be targeted by enemy skills
- Any monster can use its skills during the owner's Play Phase
- A destroyed monster leaves its slot empty

---

## Summoning

- Summoning a monster is **free** — no energy cost
- You may summon **1 monster per turn** from your hand
- You choose which empty slot to place the monster
- Summoned monsters generate energy starting the **following turn**
- Trainer cards that place monsters bypass the 1-per-turn hand rule — they are a separate action

---

## Monster Skills

- Each monster has a **skill pool** defined at card design time
- Players choose which **2 skill slots** are active in **deck settings** (configured before play, in the collection/deck-building screen)
- **Skill 1** is always available
- **Skill 2** is locked until **Mastery 1** is reached through use in duels
- Skills cost Typed Energy from the player's pool
- Skills can be used any number of times per turn while energy allows
- Mastery is tracked per card instance in the player's collection

---

## Attacking and Targeting

Skills can target:
- **Enemy monsters** — always valid while the enemy has monsters on board
- **Enemy Core directly** — only valid when the enemy has **no monsters on board**

You cannot skip enemy monsters to target their Core. Clear the board first.

---

## KO and Core Damage

When a monster's HP reaches 0:

1. The monster is destroyed and removed from the board
2. The player who dealt the killing blow deals damage to the **opponent's Core HP**
3. **Core damage = the destroyed monster's max HP**

This means:
- High-HP monsters are high-value kills — worth more Core damage
- Protecting key monsters is critical
- Clearing the enemy board opens up direct Core damage via skills

## Hybrid Cards & Deck Archetypes

> **🔴 RETIRED by the v0.3 redesign (2026-06-07) — see `DESIGN_CORE_V3.md`.**
> This entire section's premise — a monster bridging a Main+Sub *typed* energy
> cost — cannot exist once all costs are paid from one universal pool. The
> "locked decision" below is now historical only; **do not design new Hybrid
> Cards.** Kept in place so the reasoning that led here remains traceable.

> **Locked design decision — 2026-06-07.** This section resolves a design
> review that re-opened the typed-energy economy itself. See `DEV_LOG.md`
> Session 8 for the full exploration, including the paths considered and
> rejected before landing here.

### Decision: typed energy stays hard-typed — no "Any" cost will ever exist

Two alternatives were seriously explored and explicitly rejected:

- **Universal "Any" / colorless costs** (Pokémon-style, including the softer
  "1 Any + 1 [Type]" pattern) — rejected because retrofitting it onto baseline
  skill costs (e.g., changing Battle Imp's Quick Jab from "1 MIGHTY" to
  "1 Any") would hollow out the **Energy Engine** pillar: the cheapest,
  most-played skills would stop caring about type at all, leaving typed pools
  to matter only for rare expensive finishers. It also exposes a problem
  Pokémon doesn't have — Pokémon resolves "which energy pays a Colorless cost"
  for free because energy is manually attached per-card; our energy is pooled
  globally, so "Any" has no clean resolution without inventing a whole new
  cast-time pool-selection UX.
- **True multi-type "splash" play** (Magic-style multicolor decks) — rejected
  as a general deckbuilding model. Unlike Magic (where lands and spells are
  decoupled), our monsters *are* our mana base **and** occupy our 5 precious
  board slots — so multicolor play would directly tax **Board Presence**
  (pillar #1), and would multiply an already-behind balance workload
  combinatorially (only 1 of 4 Pure decks has simulation data as of this
  writing — see `DESIGN_BALANCE.md`).

**All energy costs remain hard-typed, full stop. No card will ever have an
"Any" component in its cost.**

### Two supported deck philosophies going forward

1. **Pure deck** — built entirely (or almost entirely) around one battle type.
   The original, validated shape. Maximizes synergy and consistency, minimizes
   complexity. (This is the *only* shape that exists today — all 4 starter
   decks are Pure.)
2. **Hybrid deck (Main + Sub)** — built primarily around one declared **Main**
   type, with a small number of **Hybrid Cards** (below) woven in from a
   declared **Sub** type for added texture and power.

"Soup" decks (three or more types freely mixed) are explicitly **not** a
supported target — see the rejected paths above for why that's an unmanageable
balance and comprehension surface.

### How Hybrid Cards work — "self-sufficient" bridging

A **Hybrid Card** is a monster that:
- Belongs to a **Sub type** different from the deck's declared **Main type**
- Generates its own Sub-type energy (per the standard rule — type defines what
  a monster generates)
- Has a skill whose cost mixes **Main + Sub** energy
  (e.g., "Cost: 1 MIGHTY + 1 VITAL")

**Why this avoids the "mana base" trap:** the card supplies its own secondary
fuel. Nothing else in the Main-type deck ever needs to generate Sub-type
energy — only this card does, purely for its own use. If the card dies, no
other card is left holding a stranded, unpayable cost; the requirement and its
supply are sealed inside a single self-contained unit.

**Worked example:**
```
Main deck:    MIGHTY  (deck's monsters generate MIGHTY energy)
Hybrid Card:  a VITAL-typed Fighter — generates +1 VITAL/turn
Its skill:    Cost 1 MIGHTY + 1 VITAL
              → MIGHTY portion paid from the deck's natural MIGHTY engine
              → VITAL portion paid from the energy this card generates for itself
```

### Mandatory design checks for every future Hybrid Card

Write these down and verify them *before* finalizing a Hybrid Card's numbers:

1. **Bootstrap lag must be a deliberate choice, not a surprise.** A
   newly-summoned monster doesn't generate energy until the *following* turn
   (see "Summoning" above). A Hybrid Card therefore cannot use its own hybrid
   skill the turn it lands, and may need 1-2 further turns to bank enough
   Sub-type energy depending on its generate-rate vs. its own cost. Decide
   explicitly whether that "spin-up" period is the intended tempo cost of a
   powerful bridging tool — don't let it surprise you in playtesting.
2. **Self-sufficiency ratio is a new tuning axis, separate from HP/role/cost
   balance.** A Hybrid Card's own Sub-type generate-rate must be checked
   against its own Sub-type spend-rate — especially across multiple copies
   (3 copies = 3 generators **and** 3 consumers of the same Sub-type pool).
   Generates too little relative to its cost → feels useless. Generates too
   much → produces surplus Sub-type energy the rest of the Main deck has no
   use for.
3. **"Splash tax" is a free, organic balancing force — protect it.** Slotting
   a Hybrid Card in means swapping out a Main-type card, which slows the
   deck's Main-type engine growth. This emerges naturally from existing rules
   (no new mechanic required) and is exactly the felt trade-off that makes
   "Pure vs. Hybrid" a real deckbuilding decision. Don't accidentally design it
   away later (e.g., by making Hybrid Cards feel like "free" additions on top
   of an undiminished Main-type curve).

### Target scope

| Archetype | Count | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Pure decks | 4 (one per type) | 1 of 4 simulation-validated (MIGHTY only) |
| Hybrid decks (Main × Sub, Main ≠ Sub) | up to 12 (4 × 3 ordered pairs) | 0 designed |
| **Total distinct deck identities (target)** | **16** | — |

### Recommended build order

Given the size of this balance surface relative to where validation currently
stands, build incrementally rather than all at once:

1. Finish validating all 4 Pure decks first — SWIFT/TOUGH/VITAL currently have
   **zero** simulation data (only MIGHTY-mirror has been tested; see
   `DESIGN_BALANCE.md`).
2. Introduce Hybrid Cards one bridge-card at a time, re-running simulations
   after each addition to observe its effect on the metagame.
3. Do not design all 12 Hybrid pairings up front — each one reshapes the meta
   and needs to be observed in isolation before the next is layered on.

## Card Types

### Monster
Placed onto the board. Contains:
- HP (also determines Core damage dealt to owner when killed)
- Battle type
- Energy generation rate
- Optional passive effect
- 2 active skill slots (Skill 1 always available; Skill 2 unlocks at Mastery 1)

### Trainer
One-time hand-played effect cards. No energy cost. Play from hand, resolve immediately, discard. Provide draw, energy acceleration, healing, and board setup.

### Equipment
Attach to a monster. Provide ongoing stat or effect bonuses. Equipment is destroyed when the monster it is attached to is destroyed. Play from hand, free to attach.

## Victory Condition

Each player starts with **Core HP: 30**.

Core HP is reduced when:
1. One of your monsters is killed — you take Core damage equal to that monster's max HP
2. You have no monsters on board and the opponent uses a skill against you — that skill's damage hits your Core directly

Reduce the opponent's Core HP to 0 to win.

---

## Social Experience

The game takes place inside a 3D TCG store. Players can:
- Open card packs
- Trade cards with other players
- Challenge NPCs
- Duel other players
- Participate in tournaments

Duels take place at tables. Spectators can watch naturally from outside the table area.
